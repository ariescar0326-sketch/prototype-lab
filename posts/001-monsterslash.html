Dev Log — #001 Monster Slash

3D hack-and-slash prototype. Swipe to cut monsters, chain combos, survive waves. Play instantly in your browser.

  • A mix of Fruit Ninja and music game concepts
  • Swipe across monsters to slash them
  • Built with Three.js, runs in mobile browser
  • No download, click and play
▶ PLAY

Design Notes

Prototype #001 — the very first one. The goal from day one was: someone sends you a link on LINE or iMessage, you tap it, and you're playing a 3D game within 2 seconds. No app store, no loading screen, no tutorial popup.

The idea was basically "what if Fruit Ninja was 3D and the fruits fought back?" Simple pitch. Took way longer to get right than I expected.

I built the slash mechanic first and it felt... flat. Like swiping through paper. Then I added screen shake — literally just a 3-pixel camera offset per hit — and suddenly it felt violent. One tiny change. Then combo timing happened by accident: I was testing hit detection speed and noticed fast swipes felt more satisfying, so I added scoring tiers. Perfect, Good, Miss. The tighter the timing window, the higher the score. At some point I realized it was turning into a rhythm game. Wasn't planned at all.

If I ever revisit this as a full game: procedural audio would be the big addition. Imagine each combo tier plays a different pitch, so chaining Perfects would create a rising musical scale — you'd literally be composing a melody through combat. Also want to try mixing monster types that force rhythm-switching: fast swarms vs slow tanks vs ones that dodge.

Dev Log

The screen shake rabbit hole

Spent an embarrassing amount of time tuning screen shake. Started at 10 pixels — way too much, felt like an earthquake. Went down to 1 pixel — couldn't even tell it was there. Ended up at 3px with a 150ms decay. The trick is it needs to hit your body before your brain processes what happened. Sounds dramatic for a silly game but it makes a huge difference.

Touch input on mobile is cursed

Getting swipe detection right on phones was painful. touchmove events fire at different rates depending on the device, so my slash line would sometimes have gaps in it. Had to interpolate between touch points to fill the holes. Also discovered that some Android browsers suppress touchmove if you don't call preventDefault on touchstart, which means your swipe just... doesn't register. No error, nothing. Fun.

Combo windows and feel

The timing windows are 800ms for Miss, 400ms for Good, 200ms for Perfect. I tried making Perfect tighter (100ms) but even I couldn't hit it consistently, and I built the thing. The key insight was that combo multiplier matters more than individual hit scores — it rewards sustained focus over single lucky swipes. Still not sure if the difficulty curve is right, but it feels decent for a prototype.
