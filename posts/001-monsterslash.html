Dev Log — #001 Monster Slash

3D hack-and-slash prototype. Swipe to cut monsters, chain combos, survive waves. Play instantly in your browser.

  • A mix of Fruit Ninja and music game concepts
  • Swipe across monsters to slash them
  • Built with Three.js, runs in mobile browser
  • No download, click and play
Best on mobile — Open fullscreen

Design Notes

The development goal is to create lightweight games that can be played immediately after being forwarded on a mobile phone.

Prototype #001 — First in a series of 3D mobile browser games.

The idea started from a simple question: what if Fruit Ninja was 3D and the fruits fought back?

Built the slash mechanic first — felt flat. Added screen shake, and it immediately felt better. Then added combo timing where the window gets tighter the higher your streak. It started to feel like a rhythm game. That wasn't planned, but it works.

Dev Log

Screen shake

A small camera offset on hit — just enough for the body to feel it before the eyes notice. Too much and it's nauseating, too little and it's invisible.

Combo timing

Perfect, Good, and Miss tiers. Faster slashes get stricter windows but higher scores. It gives players a goal beyond just killing — slash with precision.

What's next

There are a few directions being considered for future iterations. Procedural audio is one — having different pitch per combo tier so that chaining Perfects could form a rising scale, letting players "play" their own melody through combat. Another idea is rhythm variety through monster types: mixing fast one-hit enemies with precision-based and timing-critical ones to force constant rhythm switching.
