Dev Log — #003 Territory Conquest

4-player territory war on a 3D grid. Drag to paint, close loops to claim land. Play instantly in your browser.

  • Drag to move, close loops to claim territory
  • Roguelike Attack-path upgrades (Blade Rush, Shotgun, Bomber)
  • Procedural audio — 23 sounds, zero external files
  • No download, click and play
Best on mobile — Open fullscreen

Design Notes

Prototype #006 — A paper.io-style territory conquest game.

The core loop is simple: drag your hero to paint tiles, return home to claim enclosed land. But with 4 factions fighting over a 200x200 grid, simple becomes chaotic fast.

The roguelike skill system was originally 3 paths (Attack, Commander, Speed) with 3 tiers each. Simplified to Attack-only for a tighter experience — Blade Rush for melee cleave, Shotgun for ranged spread, Bomber for area denial with fire trails.

All 23 sound effects are procedurally generated via Web Audio API oscillators and noise. No audio files loaded at all — the entire game is a single HTML file with GLTF models.

Dev Log

Flood fill at scale

200x200 grid means 40,000 cells. Running BFS flood fill every frame would kill performance. Solution: incremental BFS that processes a fixed budget of cells per frame, spreading the work across multiple frames while keeping 60fps.

Influence map AI

Each AI faction evaluates a 50x50 downsampled influence grid to decide where to expand. Factors include enemy density, unclaimed territory value, and distance to home base. This prevents all 3 AI from dogpiling the same corner.

Procedural audio

23 distinct sounds from pure synthesis: square waves for footsteps, filtered noise for explosions, FM synthesis for level-up chimes. The audio unlock dance for mobile browsers was the trickiest part — multiple event listeners on first touch to satisfy autoplay policies.
