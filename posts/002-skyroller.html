Dev Log — #002 Sky Roller

7-player ball race on a sky track. Dodge crates, collect buffs, ragdoll physics. Play instantly in your browser.

  • Drag to steer, swipe down to roll faster
  • 3D characters with skeletal Run/Death animations
  • Physics ragdoll when you hit obstacles
  • No download, click and play
▶ PLAY

Design Notes

Prototype #002 — the ball race. Wanted to see if a 7-player race could run at 60fps in a mobile browser with actual 3D characters, ragdoll physics, and skeletal animation. Turns out: barely, but yes.

Ball size is the core design trade-off. Small balls are fast but one crate hit sends you flying off the track. Iron balls are invincible but crawl along at half speed. Every race, the AI racers get a random ball size, so no two rounds play the same. Sometimes you get a pack of small balls weaving through obstacles while one iron ball just tanks through everything.

Ragdoll was added late and almost got cut because of performance concerns. The physics engine creates separate body parts when a character gets hit — head, torso, limbs go flying. There's also a separate "launch" animation where the whole character gets sent forward along the track. Watching 3 racers get wiped out by the same crate chain reaction is genuinely the best part of the game.

Buff pads were the very last feature and ended up being the highlight. Getting launched 50 units into the sky and bouncing twice before landing gives this rollercoaster stomach-drop feeling that pure ground racing can't touch. If I did a full version, I'd lean way harder into verticality — ramps, half-pipes, maybe even upside-down track sections.

Dev Log

The SkinnedMesh clone disaster

This one drove me crazy for hours. I needed to spawn 7 copies of the same 3D character model. Naturally, I used Three.js Object3D.clone(). Models appeared, no errors in console, everything looked fine... except every character was frozen in T-pose. No animation. Turns out clone() doesn't copy bone bindings for SkinnedMesh. It's a known thing apparently, but there's zero indication anything went wrong. Had to switch to SkeletonUtils.clone which handles bones properly. Silent bugs like this are the worst.

Animation sharing doesn't work

After fixing the clone issue, animations played but only on some characters. Others would randomly freeze mid-run. The problem: I was sharing the same AnimationClips across all 7 characters' AnimationMixers. Each mixer was trying to control the same clip data, causing bind conflicts. Fix was cloning the AnimationClips too — every character needs its own complete copy of every animation. Memory-wasteful? Sure. But 7 characters times 2 animations isn't enough to matter.

Ragdoll tuning

First version of ragdoll, characters would just crumple on the spot. Boring. Added a velocity-based launch force so faster impacts send you further. Then the opposite problem: characters were flying into space at Mach 3. Ended up clamping the launch force and adding drag so the ragdoll arc looks dramatic but lands within screen view. Also added a 3-second cooldown before you can restart — forces you to watch your crash. Sounds mean but it makes each death register.
