Dev Log — #002 Sky Roller

7-player ball race on a sky track. Dodge crates, collect buffs, ragdoll physics. Play instantly in your browser.

  • Drag to steer, swipe down to roll faster
  • GLTF characters with skeletal Run/Death animations
  • Physics ragdoll when you hit obstacles
  • No download, click and play
Best on mobile — Open fullscreen

Design Notes

Prototype #002 — Second in a series of 3D mobile browser games.

Ball size is the core trade-off. Small balls are fast but one crate hit sends you flying. Iron balls are invincible but crawl along. Every race the AI gets a random mix, so the dynamics change each round.

The ragdoll uses cannon-es physics for shattered body parts and a separate flying character that launches forward along the track. Watching 3 racers get wiped out by the same crate never gets old.

Buff pads were the last addition but became the highlight — getting launched into the sky and bouncing twice before landing gives a rollercoaster feeling that pure ground racing doesn't have.

Dev Log

SkinnedMesh clone trap

Three.js Object3D.clone() doesn't copy bone bindings for SkinnedMesh. Characters were stuck in T-pose until switching to SkeletonUtils.clone. A silent bug — no error, just frozen models.

Animation binding

Each character needs its own cloned AnimationClips. Sharing clips between multiple AnimationMixers causes binding conflicts where some characters freeze while others play normally.

What makes it feel right

The game over cooldown — 3 seconds before you can restart. Forces a breath, makes each crash register. And the dust particles behind rolling balls scale with speed, so fast feels fast even before looking at the speedometer.
